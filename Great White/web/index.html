The Great White Shark

Learn More

Sharks are among Earth's most ancient animals. The fossil record dates ancestors of modern sharks to as far back as 400 million years ago. Shark species have changed relatively little during that time span and are sometimes called living fossils.The great white shark (Carcharodon carcharias), also known as the great white, white shark or white pointer, is a species of large mackerel shark which can be found in the coastal surface waters of all the major oceans. They generally prefer to live in cooler waters that have temperatures ranging from 54-75Â° Fahrenheit. Great White Sharks cannot be kept in captivity because they become disoriented and will stop eating and continuously run into the aquarium walls until they die.

  • Great White Sharks live and hunt on the coast of every continent in the world the except Antarctica.

  • Scientists previously thought the life expectancy of a Great White Shark was around 25 years, but a recent study shows that their life expectancy is actually around 70 years.

  • Great White Sharks weigh an average of 1,500-2,400 pounds at maturity, about one ton..

  • The heaviest Great White Shark ever recorded in the wild was estimated to weigh in at a whopping 7,328 pounds and eat average of 11 tons of food..

  • Though an apex predator, Great White Sharks are often attacked and eaten by Killer Whales.

  • Although the great white shark is known as the most ferocious and aggressive shark, this shark typically does not target humans as prey.
